### Grammar Points

#### 1. The Particle ～のに (Noni)
Used to express contrast or a sense of "despite" or "even though." It often carries a tone of regret or frustration, which fits the theme of a bittersweet breakup.

*   **Example:** 好きだ**のに**、言えなかった。
*   **Romaji:** *Suki da noni, ienakatta.*
*   **English:** Even though I loved them, I couldn't say it.

#### 2. The Potential Form + できない (Dekinai)
In emotional songs, the potential form is frequently used to describe things the speaker *wishes* they could do but cannot, highlighting a sense of helplessness.

*   **Example:** 忘れようとしても、忘れ**られない**。
*   **Romaji:** *Wasureyou to shitemo, wasurerarenai.*
*   **English:** Even if I try to forget, I cannot forget.

#### 3. ～はずがない (Hazu ga nai)
This structure expresses a strong negative conviction: "it cannot be that..." or "there is no way that..." It is used when the speaker is denying a hopeful possibility.

*   **Example:** そんなハッピーエンドになる**はずがない**。
*   **Romaji:** *Sonna happii endo ni naru hazu ga nai.*
*   **English:** There is no way it will result in a happy ending like that.
