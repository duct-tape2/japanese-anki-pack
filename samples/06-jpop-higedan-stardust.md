### Grammar Points

#### 1. The Use of 〜てしまう (-te shimau)
This structure is frequently used in J-pop to express regret or the completion of an action that the speaker didn't necessarily want to happen. It adds an emotional layer of "unfortunately" or "completely."

*   **Example Sentence:** 夢を忘れてしまいました。
*   **Romaji:** *Yume o wasurete shimaimashita.*
*   **English:** I have (unfortunately) forgotten my dream.

#### 2. The Particle だけ (dake) for Limitation
In songs about longing or distance, *dake* is used to emphasize that something is the "only" thing remaining or the "only" thing desired.

*   **Example Sentence:** あなたの声だけが聞こえます。
*   **Romaji:** *Anata no koe dake ga kikoemasu.*
*   **English:** I can hear only your voice.

#### 3. Nominalizing Verbs with こと (koto)
To talk about abstract concepts like "the act of dreaming" or "the thing about loving," Japanese speakers turn verbs into nouns using *koto*.

*   **Example Sentence:** 諦めることは簡単です。
*   **Romaji:** *Akirameru koto wa kantan desu.*
*   **English:** Giving up is easy.
